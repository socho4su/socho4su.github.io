Structural Problems

A strong Student Union is strengthened by students who are well aware of what is going on in the SU and are not apathetic to developments within their community. A strong Student Union is made up of extensive and varied representation among all demographics of Stuyvesant.

Stuyvesant Student's Forum

Not a single Stuyvesant Student’s Forum meeting was held this year. Matthew & Tahseen truly believe that if the large organizations of Stuyvesant are more involved in the Student Union, we can work together to promote extensive progress throughout Stuyvesant. However, the Student’s Forum is always pushed to the back of the mind when there is ever an issue that arises. Thus, Matthew & Tahseen will amend the Constitution to give the Stuyvesant Student's Forum voting privileges with the SU Executive Council. This will work to ensure that the Student Forum is more intricately involved within the work of the Student Union & can contribute to further development of solutions within Stuyvesant.

Fighting Apathy

There is only one way to fight apathy. It’s to convince the people of Stuyvesant that the Student Union truly holds a stand when it comes to school policy. The lower the apathy is, the stronger of a presence the SU can have when lobbying the administration. We’ve noticed a general correlation when it comes to how responsive a grade is and how extensive its communications system is. Thus, Matthew & Tahseen will implement their 3-Step Communication Plan across the board. It is essential that we get the support from the incoming freshmen as well. Thus we will set up Incoming Freshmen Mailing List signups at Camp Stuy. Freshmen usually have the most unique ideas and this will be a great way to start fighting apathy from the base.

So & Chowdhury for SU

So & Chowdhury will serve to improve student life with respect to education, extra-curricular activities, and other areas of school life. We will work to ensure that the voice of the student body is heard in all discussion of school policy. Under So & Chowdhury, no student who truly shows an interest in investing their time to benefit the Stuyvesant Community will be turned away from the Student Union.
