Communication & Representation

When an unfair problem arises with a school policy we need to respond quickly. That means communicating the problem to you immediately, getting a response, and then accurately representing your opinion. A strong foundation is essential.

Brushing Up On The Foundation We've Built

  • Websites for all Caucuses
  • Professional Email Servers for all Caucuses
  • Mailing Lists for all Caucuses
  • Caucus Hotline for all Caucuses
  • Online Calendars for all Caucuses

We've spent months establishing these systems of communication. accross the board, but there have been technical glitches. We're going to work with our IT Department for a faster and smoother system next year.

Approaching You & Getting Your Thoughts

Steps To Success

  1. Set up a committee dedicated to this cause.
  2. Have monthly sessions when this committee will talk to students to figure out what's bothering them.
  3. Have monthly meetings after every session to figure out what can and can't be done and set our goals.
  4. Develop a plan to execute the initiative.
  5. Execute it!

Mass Emailing The Entire School

We've worked extremely hard to gain access to all emails this year, and we did just that. We have a list of every single Stuyvesant Student @stuy.edu email address, but we can't use them.
Our IT Department simply did not have the time or resources to develop a platform to handle this volume of emails. We're going to spent the entire summer building a platform, and if need be, we'll purchase resources to assist us in doing so.

So & Chowdhury for SU

So & Chowdhury will serve to improve student life with respect to education, extra-curricular activities, and other areas of school life. We will work to ensure that the voice of the student body is heard in all discussion of school policy. Under So & Chowdhury, no student who truly shows an interest in investing their time to benefit the Stuyvesant Community will be turned away from the Student Union.
